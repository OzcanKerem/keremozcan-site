About Me

Electrical and Electronics Engineer focused on research-driven engineering, spanning digital systems, photonics, and applied hardware development.

Kerem Özcan

Electrical & Electronics Engineer • M.Sc. Student @ Ankara Yıldırım Beyazıt University

Background

I am an Electrical and Electronics Engineer currently pursuing my M.Sc. at Ankara Yıldırım Beyazıt University (AYBÜ). My academic and engineering work sits at the intersection of digital hardware, communication systems, and emerging computational paradigms. I aim to build a strong foundation that combines rigorous analysis with practical implementation.

Research Interests

My primary research interests include quantum circuits and related modeling approaches, FPGA-based digital system design, and advanced circuit design methodologies. I am particularly interested in how modern hardware platforms can accelerate computation and signal processing workflows, and how design choices impact performance, robustness, and implementation complexity.

I also focus on advanced optoelectronic applications and optical fiber communication systems, where I explore device/system concepts and signal integrity considerations. These domains motivate practical questions around modulation, detection, noise behavior, and end-to-end system reliability.

Applied Engineering

Alongside academic research, I actively work with embedded systems, circuit design, and applied engineering problems. I have hands-on experience with smart home technologies, solar energy systems, and automatic door and access control systems, combining field expertise with solid engineering foundations. In parallel, I maintain an active interest in amateur radio technologies and RF systems.

How I Work

I value structured engineering: clear requirements, measurable targets, careful verification, and clean documentation. Whether the work is academic or industrial, I prefer practical outcomes that can be tested, reproduced, and improved iteratively. I write short technical notes to capture key assumptions, decisions, and results for future reuse.
